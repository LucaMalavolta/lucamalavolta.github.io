August 20, 2019 Luca Malavolta

I’m an observational astronomer focused on detecting and characterizing planetary systems orbiting nearby stars. I use photometry from space and high-resolution spectropy from the ground to measure masses and radii of exoplanets, and constrain their bulk compositions. I am curious about how planets form and evolve with time, the frequency of planetary systems in young Open Clusters and Associations, and the prospects for detecting a Earth twin close to our Solar System. There's a good chance you'll meet me at some telescope at the end of an observing run!

Currently I'm a Researcher at INAF-Observatory of Catania. I'm involved in the PLATO mission, and while I wait for this amazing mission to discover the first true Earth analog, I try to understand how the reduce stellar activity in order to measure the masses of small planets.
