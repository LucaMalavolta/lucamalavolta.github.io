PLATO

Planetary Transits and Oscillations of Stars

PLATO is the ESA M3 mission in the Cosmic Vision 2015-2025 for the search of Earth-sized planets inthe habitable zone of Sun-like stars, and the precise characterization of stars with asteroseismology.
In PLATO, I am Leader of WP 131 250 (Large Spectroscopic Catalogue) and WP 131 320 (Spectroscopic Data) and also member of WPs 111 000 (Coordination of Tools for Lightcurve Filtering) and 115 100 (Astrophysical Noise Source). In this project I’m bringing my expertise in large spectroscopic datasets and the detection of planets in presence of stellar activity, with the ultimate goal of discovering the first true Earth analog.

HARPS-N GTO

Guaranteed Time of Observations

The HARPS-N spectrograph is a high-precision radial-velocity instrument similar to HARPS and located in the Northern hemisphere, at the Telescopio Nazionale Galieo on La Palma (Canary Islands, Spain). The main scientific rationale of HARPS-N is the characterization and discovery of terrestrial planets by combining transits and Doppler measurements, focusing especially on mass and composition of super-Earths and mini-Neptunes. The HARPS-N Project is a collaboration between the Astronomical Observatory of the Geneva University (lead), the CfA in Cambridge, the Universities of St. Andrews and Edinburgh, the Queens University of Belfast, and the TNG-INAF Observatory.

GAPS

Global Architecture of Planetary Systems

After the successful Global Architecture of Planetary Systems (GAPS) project, the Italian exoplanets community has gathered together again to exploit the unique capabilities GIARPS@TNG. In GAPS2 we shift our focus from the detection and characterization of planets to the study of the origin of planetary systems. We are investigating migration mechanisms by looking for planets around young (< 0.1 Gyr) and intermediate-age (< 0.7 Gyr) stars. We are tracing the original formation site and path to migration of planets by looking for sodium (VIS), water, titanium monoxide and other molecules (NIR) in the atmospheres of the largest sample of hot transiting planets to-date.
